Reflection: The Brief History of Web Robots

27 June 2020

“An internet bot is a software application that performs automated tasks by running scripts over the internet.” (Knecht, 2020)

Bots perform simple structurally repetitive tasks at a far greater speed than humans could ever achieve. For the most part, these bots are harmless and are, in fact, crucial for making the internet as valuable and useful as it is. However, like most things that exist on the internet, they can become destructive and malignant when used by criminals or people who have bad intentions

The oldest internet bot traces back to 1988 with the rise of internet chat relay also known as IRC. Some of the first bots used in IRC were Jyrki Alakuijala’s Puppe, Greg Linghal’s Game Manager (Hunt the Wumpus Game) and Bill Wisner’s Bartender. The bots used on IRC at the time, provided automated services to users and sat in the channel’s to make sure that the servers didn’t close it down because it was inactive.

Other bots in the earlier history of the internet were web crawlers that were used for the first search engines. The first bot that was used to index webpages was WebCrawler and this was in 1994. The most famous web crawler, known today as the Googlebot, was originally called BackRub when it was created in 1996.

Some of the earliest botnet programs were Sub7 and Pretty Park, a Trojan and worm. They were released into the IRC network in 1999, Their purpose or their function was to install themselves onto the users machine when they connected to an IRC channel so they could listen for malicious commands.

GTbot was a fake mIRC client that emerged in IRC in 2000. It was capable of some of the first denial of service attacks. In the years that followed, botnet creators were able to use the machine infected from this to enact various attacks ranging from ransomware to identity theft and so on. After a while, botnets left IRC and began communicating through HTTP, SSL and ICMP.

The prevalence of botnets has grown since then and still continues to. However, as important as they’ve become, they have also become a great tool to hackers. In 2007, one of the biggest botnets, Storm, infected up to 50 million computers, all of which were then used to commit a multitude of crimes including stock price fraud and identity theft. Botnets also played a significant role in the spam email epidemic in 2009, in which a botnet program called Cutwail was used to send 74 billion spam emails a day.

Today, bots are an important component to the construction of the internet that we experience today. Web crawlers allow for most the useful information to be found quicker amidst the millions of possible results. Chat bots have are a vital component for the operation of chat rooms and dialog windows and have evolved to the point of being able to fool humans, like the Cleverbot. Bots are essential to the working of the internet and while this is something that is considered essential, they, in hands of people with ill intentions, could also be destructive.
